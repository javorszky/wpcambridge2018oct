Balance!

How not to piss off people

1. Tracking

as little as you can get away with

Tracking adds javascript, javascript adds weight, complexity, etc...

People also don't like being tracked. And I'm blocking your tracking scripts anyways...

2. Have the simplest design you can get away with

No, you do not need your extra font.

No, you do not need fancy transitions.

No, you do not need 26 Mb images.

3. Accessibility!

  • Do not ignore people with disabilities!
  • ALL different disabilities: sight, sound, motor, cognitive
  • accessibility is also for people who have changed circumstances that are NOT disabilities, for example: mothers with 3 screaming children

4. No, seriously. Accessibility. Start treating it as a necessary part of your sites!

5. Do you have an accessible site to at least A standard?

preferably AA

AAA?

https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/helping-people-to-use-your-service/making-your-service-accessible-an-introduction

6. Tech

Have an actually competent DNS registrar and host.

Use HTTPS.

more importantly, DO NOT USE HTTP.

No, it's not faster than HTTPS.

Yes, even on the pages that have no payment / ecommerce.

YES, even on the sites that are just blogs.

7. Tech

  • Containers!
  • Shove all your media off-site (S3)
  • You can still use those while developing locally
  • No need to download 70 Gb!

8. Dev

  • No shared accounts. Devs get their own details. Better yet, they get invited to accounts.
  • Revokation.
  • What's your playbook if you don't want to work with someone anymore?

9. more Dev

  • Backups! Have you tested restoration?
  • version control system
  • automated deployment. Copying to sftp is very 90s.
  • Testing?

10. even more Dev

  • When deploying, how long does your site go down?
  • Atomic deployments
  • Multi-server environments

11. last Dev

  • clean code / code style used / code linting used
  • Modern programming principles (composer)
  • .env files!
